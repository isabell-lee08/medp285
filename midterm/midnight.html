THE DEEP SEA

Midnight

Also called the bathypelagic zone, reaching depths of 4000 meters. No light passes these depths, having long been absorbed. In many places, the midnight zone hits the sea floor. The zone hovers around a perpetual 4° Celsius / 39° Fahrenheit. It is home to 70% of all life in the sea.

What lives here?

Very little is known about the creatures that live here. Bioluminescence is common, and the microbial environment is very unique.

Anglerfish...

have a bioluminescent lure fixed to their heads, which helps them attract prey. Female anglerfish are much larger than male anglerfish.

Blobfish...

are famously ugly, but they only appear blobby due to changes in surrounding pressure. Otherwise, they look like any other fish.

THE DEEP SEA

Twilight

Also called the mesopelagic or midwater zone, this level reaches 1000 meters below the surface.

There is very little sunlight this deep down. Temperatures begin rapidly decreasing with depth, as this level includes the thermocline. The mixed water of varying temperatures transitions into the deeper, colder water of the deep sea.

What lives here?

Despite the extreme depths, this zone still harbors many life forms. Creatures this deep down begin to have bioluminescence to compensate for the lack of light.

Squid...

come in many shapes and sizes. The colossal squid can grow over 14 meters long.

Zooplankton...

are tiny organisms that provide food for many other creatures.

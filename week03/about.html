TYPES of BLUE

This page features a noncomprehensive list of some shades, forms, and names blue can take.

This Wikipedia page details a list of varying blues; for personal purposes, I am including colors in my index that are technically variants of azure or cyan.

  • #0000FF blue

    This blue's hexadecimal code denotes a color with 0% red value, 0% green value, and 100% blue value. When displayed on a webpage, it is an incredibly intense shade of blue.

  • Phthalo blue

    Also known as copper phthalocyanine, it is a widely used deep blue pigment. Commonly used by artists in painting, the pigment can be found in numerous products, such as shampoo and tattoo ink.

  • Lapis lazuli

    A vividly blue gemstone banded by streaks of gray and gold. It is significant as a historically prized material and can be made into ultramarine pigment.

  • Blue faience

    Faience is an ancient form of ceramic glazed and designed in vibrant colors. Faience can come in many colors, including white, brown, and pink, but a deep, dark blue is very popular to work with. Egyptian faience bears famously striking and bright blue-green hues.

  • Glaucous blue

    A dull, dusty blue color that borders on gray, potentially with hints of green. Glaucous itself is an adjective referring to the waxy, "powdery bloom" you'd find on grapes.

  • Alice blue

    A very pale and gentle blue, with hints of green, that borders on white. It's also referred to as an icy blue. The color of this text is alice blue!

  • Han blue

    A deep blue pigment used in ancient China, alongside Han purple. Han blue can be naturally found as the blue mineral effenbergerite, though Han blue is structurally more stable.

  • Zaffre blue

    A relatively dark blue pigment that comes from cobalt, and was often used to stain glass.

  • Bluebonnets

    Bluebonnet flowers include any purple, blue, or otherwise similarly-colored flower from the genus Lupinus. These flowers range from soft purple hues to striking indigos; they are the state flower of Texas.

  • YInMn blue

    Also known as Oregon blue or Mas blue, YInMn blue is an intensely vibrant artificial pigment. It's made up of yttrium, indium, manganese, and oxygen, hence its name.

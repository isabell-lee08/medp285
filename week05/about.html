Across cultures

Today, the constellations we are most used to are mostly drawn from ancient Greek origins. Ancient Egypt, ancient China, Native American societies, and Austrailian aboriginals, are among many cultures to have their own constellations, all with different stories.

The Wintermaker is an Ojibwe constellation, a Native American peoples. It is comprised of many similar stars to Orion, as well as some stars from surrounding constellations. The Wintermaker, Biboonkeonini, is a canoe man who rules over winter, appearing overhead during the winter months.

In science

There are 88 officially recognized constellations used in modern astronomy. They are used to chart the skies, which help with identifying celestial phenomena (such as meteor showers). The stars are also useful for navigation; the slow-moving stars have helped people find their way across long distances since antiquity.

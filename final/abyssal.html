THE DEEP SEA

Abyssal

Also called the abyssopelagic zone, ending 6000 meters below the surface. The word "abyss" has Greek origins, meaning "bottomless."

The abyssal zone is the largest environment on the planet, comprised of our sea floors and covering around 60% of the Earth's surface. It is a severe environment characterized by freezing temperatures and immense pressures.

What lives here?

Being among the largest environments on Earth, many strange and undiscovered creatures find themselves at home here.

Dumbo octopus...

are the deepest-living octopus species. They are named for their characteristic ears.

Cusk eel...

are not truly eels, but bony fish. Some of the deepest reported fish are cusk eels.
